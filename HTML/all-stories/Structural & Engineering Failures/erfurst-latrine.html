THE ERFURT LATRINE DISASTER

A Royal Gathering: In July 1184, King Henry VI of Germany called for a Hoftag (informal assembly) at the Petersburg Citadel in Erfurt. The meeting was intended to mediate a bitter feud between Archbishop Conrad of Mainz and Landgrave Louis III of Thuringia. The event attracted high-ranking nobles, knights, and church officials from across the Holy Roman Empire, all of whom crowded into the second story of the provost's lodge of St. Mary's Cathedral to witness the negotiations.

/>

The Floor Gives Way: The sheer weight of dozens of armored nobles and their retinues proved too much for the ancient wooden floorboards of the upper room. Suddenly, the central floor collapsed. The falling nobles crashed through the first floor as well, plunging directly into the large, communal cesspit (latrine) located in the basement of the building.


Key Facts

Date: July 1184

Location: Erfurt, Holy Roman Empire

Casualties: Approximately 60 high-ranking nobles and knights; many drowned or suffocated.

Cause: A structural collapse caused by the weight of armored nobles and their retinues.

The Choking Toll: While the fall itself killed several men, the majority of the casualties were far more gruesome. Approximately 60 high-ranking nobles and knights drowned or suffocated in the liquid human waste of the cesspit. Among the dead were Count Gozmar of Ziegenhain and Count Friedrich of Abenberg. King Henry VI and Archbishop Conrad only survived because they were sitting in a stone-walled alcove at the edge of the room, clinging to the window railings until rescuers could reach them with ladders.

Lesson: The Erfurt Latrine Disaster remains one of the most bizarre and undignified tragedies in medieval history. It serves as a stark example of the limitations of medieval engineering and the dangers of overcrowding wooden structures not designed for concentrated loads. It also highlights how a single structural failure could alter the political landscape of an entire region by wiping out a significant portion of its ruling class in a matter of seconds.
