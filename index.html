Lake Nyos Overturn

On August 21, 1986, a limnic eruption at Lake Nyos in Cameroon released 1.6 million tons of CO₂ — an invisible cloud that swept through surrounding valleys and claimed 1,746 lives before dawn.

Great Smog of 1952

On December 5, 1952, an anticyclone trapped cold, stagnant air over London, mixing coal smoke into a sulfuric acid mist so dense that buses were abandoned mid-road and theater performances were canceled. By December 9, the smog had silently claimed an estimated 12,000 lives.

Banqiao Dam Failure

On August 8, 1975, Super Typhoon Nina dropped a year's worth of rain in 24 hours, overwhelming China's "Iron Dam." With sluice gates clogged and communication lines severed, a 20-foot wall of water surged out, triggering a chain collapse of 62 dams and an estimated 171,000 to 230,000 deaths.

The Holodomor

In the winter of 1932–33, Stalin's Soviet government seized every scrap of food from Ukrainian villages, set impossible grain quotas, and blocked starving families from fleeing — all while exporting grain abroad. Between 3.9 and 7 million people perished in a famine engineered by policy, not drought.

Tangshan Earthquake

At 3:42 AM on July 28, 1976, a magnitude 7.8 earthquake struck the sleeping city of Tangshan, China, collapsing 85% of its buildings in under 30 seconds. With no warning and all aid refused on political grounds, an estimated 655,000 people perished — making it the deadliest earthquake of the 20th century.

Uncovering Forgotten and Buried Tragedies

Stragedy is a digital archive dedicated to bringing forgotten global tragedies into the light by uncovering stories that have been sidelined for too long. We dig deep into historical records to investigate exactly what happened and why these events took place, ensuring that hidden narratives are finally given the attention they deserve.

The name itself is a mix of "strategy" and "tragedy," our mission is to explore the specific strategies used to manage each crisis. We go beyond the surface of a disaster to examine the decision-making and planning that occurred in the heat of the moment. By studying these historical strategies, we uncover the lessons learned from the past and shed light on the events that time almost forgot.
